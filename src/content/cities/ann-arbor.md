Ann Arbor is known as "Tree Town" — and for good reason. The city maintains over 40,000 street trees and 7,000 park trees across more than 200 species, with a canopy coverage of about 33% and a goal of reaching 40%. It's a city that takes its trees seriously, from the 10,000 Trees Initiative (completed six years ahead of schedule) to its longstanding Tree City USA designation.

## Tree challenges in Ann Arbor

Ann Arbor's mature urban canopy creates specific challenges for homeowners:

- **Emerald ash borer** — first discovered in Southeast Michigan in 2002, EAB has devastated ash trees across the region. Many properties still have standing dead ash that needs safe removal.
- **Oak wilt risk** — oaks should never be pruned between April 15 and July 15 to prevent the spread of this deadly fungal disease transmitted by sap-feeding beetles.
- **Mature maples near structures** — Ann Arbor's dominant maple canopy means many older homes have large maples growing close to rooflines, foundations, and driveways.
- **Norway maple management** — the city is actively reducing this over-represented invasive species, which can crowd out native trees.

## Ann Arbor's tree ordinance

Ann Arbor has Chapter 40 (Trees and Other Vegetation) in its city code. Trees on private property that are diseased, damaged, or insect-infested and present hazards to public property must be addressed. Trees must be maintained so no part intrudes on the public right-of-way below 8 feet. Violations can result in fines up to $1,000.

We recommend homeowners check with the city regarding permit requirements before scheduling tree removal. Our team can advise on the general process during your free assessment.

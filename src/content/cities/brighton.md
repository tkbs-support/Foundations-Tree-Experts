Brighton sits at the edge of Foundations' service area in Livingston County — a community defined by its lakes, wooded lots, and a landscape that blends small-town downtown with lakefront living and newer suburban development.

Foundations Tree Experts serves Brighton homeowners with tree removal, trimming, stump grinding, and lot clearing. We travel to Brighton regularly for larger jobs and emergency work.

## Tree care in Brighton

Brighton's landscape creates specific tree care needs that differ from the denser urban canopy of Ann Arbor or Canton:

- **Lakefront properties** — large trees on waterfront lots can threaten docks, seawalls, and lake access. Removal near water requires careful planning.
- **Wooded subdivisions** — many Brighton neighborhoods were carved from wooded land, leaving mature trees close to homes.
- **Oak-heavy lots** — Brighton's landscape has significant oak presence, requiring awareness of oak wilt timing restrictions (no pruning April through July).
- **Emerald ash borer** — Livingston County has been impacted by EAB, and many properties still have dead or declining ash trees.

## Brighton Township tree regulations

Brighton Township's zoning ordinance includes design regulations for tree preservation, particularly for new subdivision and condominium development. Trees planted along pedestrian routes must meet specific height requirements.

The City of Brighton maintains a tree ordinance on file with the Michigan DNR.

We recommend checking with your municipality before scheduling tree removal on your property.

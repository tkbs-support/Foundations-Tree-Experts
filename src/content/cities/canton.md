Canton Township is one of Michigan's largest townships by population, with a landscape that ranges from established 1970s subdivisions with mature trees to newer developments and 134 acres of protected woodlands and wetlands. It's a community where tree care matters — from the towering pines of Sunflower Village to the walkable streets of Cherry Hill Village.

Foundations Tree Experts serves Canton homeowners, builders, and developers with full tree removal, trimming, stump grinding, and lot clearing services.

## Neighborhoods we serve in Canton

We work throughout Canton Township, including:

- **Sunflower Village** — large subdivision north of Ford Road with 70 acres of common areas, mature pines, oaks, and ornamental trees
- **Cherry Hill Village** — new urbanist neighborhood in western Canton with walkable streets and historic architecture
- **Pheasant Run area** — includes Pheasant View, Fairway Pines, and Fairways at Pheasant Run near the golf course
- **Royal Pointe** — established 1990s homes at Beck and Warren roads
- **Coventry Gardens** and **Forest Trails** — residential subdivisions with established tree cover

## Tree challenges in Canton

Canton's mix of older subdivisions and newer development creates varied tree care needs:

- **Emerald ash borer** — Wayne County was ground zero for the EAB discovery in 2002. Many Canton properties still have dead or declining ash trees that need removal, particularly in older subdivisions planted heavily with ash.
- **Mature subdivision trees** — neighborhoods like Sunflower Village (built in the 1970s-1990s) have trees that are now 30-50 years old, reaching sizes that can threaten structures and overwhelm residential lots.
- **New-build lot clearing** — Canton continues to develop, and builders need cleared sites for new construction. We work with developers on site prep and selective clearing.
- **Protected woodlands** — Canton has 134 acres of designated Nature Area preserving woodlands and wetlands, and trees adjacent to these areas may have additional considerations.

## Canton's tree ordinance

Canton Township has a tree protection ordinance that requires permits for removing trees 6 inches DBH or greater. Key details:

- **Occupied lots under 2 acres are exempt** from the permit requirement
- Landmark trees and trees within forests have additional protections
- Clear cutting within the drip line of a forest on property over 2 acres requires a permit
- Replacement trees are required when more than 20% of total drip line area is removed
- Agricultural operations, nurseries, and utility companies are also exempt

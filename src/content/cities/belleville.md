Belleville is a small city in Wayne County centered around Belleville Lake, with the surrounding Van Buren Township making up much of the broader community. The area blends lakefront living with established suburban neighborhoods and rural properties — each with its own tree care needs.

Foundations Tree Experts serves Belleville and Van Buren Township with tree removal, trimming, stump grinding, and 24/7 emergency response.

## Areas we serve in Belleville

We work throughout Belleville and Van Buren Township, including:

- **Belleville Lake waterfront** — shoreline properties around the lake, where tree work requires careful planning around docks, seawalls, and lake access
- **Savage Road area** — established residential properties with mature trees south of Belleville Lake
- **Rawsonville Road corridor** — mix of residential and rural properties along this main north-south route through the community
- **Tyler Road / Quirk Road** — suburban subdivisions in Van Buren Township with aging tree canopy from original plantings
- **Belleville Road corridor** — homes and properties along the route connecting to I-94, including both older neighborhoods and newer development
- **Van Buren Township rural** — larger lots on the western and southern edges of the township with significant woodlots and hedgerows

## Tree challenges in Belleville

Belleville's mix of waterfront, suburban, and rural settings creates varied tree care needs:

- **Lakefront trees** — Belleville Lake is the centerpiece of the community, and waterfront properties have large shade trees that can threaten docks, seawalls, and views. Willows, cottonwoods, and silver maples are common lakefront species that grow fast and can become structurally weak.
- **Aging subdivision trees** — established Van Buren Township neighborhoods have trees from original plantings that are now 30–50+ years old. These mature trees can develop structural problems, outgrow their lots, or decline and need removal.
- **Emerald ash borer** — Wayne County has been heavily impacted by EAB since its discovery in Southeast Michigan in 2002. Dead and declining ash remain on many properties and should be removed before they fall.
- **Oak wilt risk** — present across Southeast Michigan. Oaks should not be pruned between April and July to prevent the spread of this fungal disease.
- **Storm exposure** — the open terrain around Belleville Lake and the surrounding rural land leave properties exposed to wind damage from seasonal storms. We respond 24/7.

## Growing community, growing tree care needs

Belleville and Van Buren Township continue to see residential growth, with new subdivisions and infill development alongside established neighborhoods. New homeowners inherit mature trees that need professional assessment, and new builds require site clearing. Whether it's a hazardous lakefront willow, a dead ash in a Tyler Road subdivision, or lot clearing for new construction, we bring the same professional approach to every job.

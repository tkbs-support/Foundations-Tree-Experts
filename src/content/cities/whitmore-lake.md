Whitmore Lake is a lakeside community spanning Northfield Township in Washtenaw County and Green Oak Township in Livingston County. The area is defined by Whitmore Lake itself — a 670-acre all-sports lake surrounded by waterfront homes, rolling rural land, and the natural beauty that draws residents to this part of Southeast Michigan.

Foundations Tree Experts serves the Whitmore Lake area with tree removal, trimming, stump grinding, and emergency response. We're a short drive north of Ann Arbor and work in the area regularly.

## Areas we serve around Whitmore Lake

We work throughout the Whitmore Lake community and surrounding townships, including:

- **Whitmore Lake waterfront** — shoreline properties around the lake, where tree work requires careful planning around docks, seawalls, and lake access
- **East Shore Drive / West Shore Drive** — lakefront homes with mature trees growing close to the water and neighboring structures
- **Whitmore Lake Road corridor** — residential properties along the main road connecting to Ann Arbor
- **Northfield Township rural** — larger lots along Seven Mile Road, Five Mile Road, and Pontiac Trail with significant hardwood cover
- **US-23 corridor** — properties near the highway interchange area, including newer subdivisions and established homes

## Tree challenges around Whitmore Lake

The Whitmore Lake area has distinct tree care needs shaped by the lake and surrounding landscape:

- **Lakefront trees** — trees along the shoreline can threaten docks, seawalls, and views. Removal near water requires careful planning, and sometimes crane-assisted lifting to protect the shoreline. Large willows and cottonwoods are common waterfront species that grow fast and can become hazardous.
- **Rural acreage** — many properties in Northfield Township sit on multi-acre lots with dense tree cover, requiring periodic pruning, hazard tree removal, or selective clearing for improvements.
- **Storm exposure** — open rural terrain and lakefront exposure make Whitmore Lake properties vulnerable to wind damage from seasonal storms. We respond 24/7.
- **Emerald ash borer** — Washtenaw County has been impacted by EAB since 2002. Many properties still have standing dead ash that should be removed before they fall.
- **Oak wilt risk** — present across Southeast Michigan. Oaks should not be pruned between April and July.

## A lake community that values its trees

Whitmore Lake's appeal is inseparable from its natural setting. The mature trees lining the shore and surrounding properties are part of what makes the community attractive. Responsible tree care — knowing when to prune, when to remove, and how to protect the trees worth keeping — matters here more than most places.

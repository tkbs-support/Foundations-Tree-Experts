Plymouth is a city that takes its leafy character seriously. From the 300+ year-old sugar maple in Miller Woods to the mature street trees lining downtown, trees are central to Plymouth's identity. The community even has its own nonprofit — Keep Plymouth Leafy — dedicated to growing and maintaining the urban tree canopy.

Foundations Tree Experts serves Plymouth homeowners with the full range of tree care services, from routine pruning to emergency storm response. We understand the value Plymouth places on its trees and approach every job with that in mind.

## Neighborhoods we serve in Plymouth

We work throughout Plymouth, including:

- **Downtown Plymouth** — historic buildings and mature street trees along walkable shopping and dining districts
- **Old Village** — one of Plymouth's oldest neighborhoods with well-preserved historic homes and established tree canopy
- **Beacon Meadows** — family-friendly with well-maintained parks and sidewalks

- **Trailwood** — established subdivision with mature plantings
- **Andover Lakes** — waterfront lots around a small lake with large trees

## Tree challenges in Plymouth

Plymouth's historic character and mature tree canopy create specific challenges:

- **Emerald ash borer** — Wayne County has been heavily impacted by EAB since its discovery in 2002. Dead and declining ash trees remain on many Plymouth properties.
- **Aging trees near historic homes** — many of Plymouth's older neighborhoods have trees planted at the same time as the homes. After decades of growth, these trees can encroach on rooflines, crack foundations, and block driveways.
- **Oak wilt risk** — oaks should not be pruned between April 15 and July 15 to prevent the spread of this fungal disease. Plymouth's oak population is significant, particularly in and around Miller Woods.
- **Dutch Elm Disease legacy** — while most American elms in Michigan were lost to DED, some survived (notably in Miller Woods). Remaining elms require monitoring and proper care.

## Plymouth's tree preservation ordinance

Plymouth has a detailed tree preservation code (Chapter 8.20) that homeowners should be aware of:

- Permits are required before removing **Heritage or Protected Trees**
- Street trees cannot be removed unless an arborist determines the tree is dead, diseased, or dying — and the property owner pays for the assessment
- **Replacement trees are required** for each tree removed
- Violations: first offense up to **$750**, second within a year up to **$850**, third or more up to **$1,000**

We recommend checking with the city before scheduling any removal. Our team can advise during your free on-site assessment.

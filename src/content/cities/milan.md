Milan is a small city in southern Washtenaw County — extending into Monroe County — with a mix of in-town residential streets and surrounding rural properties. The area's agricultural heritage means many lots have significant tree cover, from mature hardwoods on older properties to volunteer growth on former farmland.

Foundations Tree Experts serves Milan with tree removal, trimming, stump grinding, and emergency response. We're based in the Ann Arbor area and work in Milan regularly.

## Areas we serve in Milan

We work throughout the City of Milan and surrounding Milan Township, including:

- **Downtown Milan** — older homes along Main Street, County Street, and Wabash Street with mature street trees that have been growing for decades
- **Hurd Road area** — residential properties on the north side of town transitioning into larger rural lots
- **Platt Road corridor** — mix of established homes and newer development south of the city center
- **Arkona Road / Cone Road** — rural properties on acreage with significant woodlots and hedgerows
- **Milan Township** — agricultural and rural-residential properties surrounding the city, many with large trees along property lines and driveways

## Tree challenges in Milan

Milan's blend of small-town neighborhoods and surrounding rural land creates varied tree care needs:

- **Aging trees on older lots** — Milan's established in-town neighborhoods have trees planted when the homes were built. Many are now reaching the end of their lifespan or have outgrown their lots, growing into power lines, rooflines, and foundations.
- **Rural property maintenance** — larger lots outside Milan's city limits often have extensive tree cover requiring periodic pruning, hazard tree removal, or lot clearing for improvements and outbuildings.
- **Emerald ash borer** — Washtenaw and Monroe counties have been impacted by EAB since 2002. Dead and declining ash remain on many properties and should be removed before they fall.
- **Oak wilt risk** — present across Southeast Michigan. Oaks should not be pruned between April and July to prevent the spread of this fungal disease.
- **Storm exposure** — rural and semi-rural properties are exposed to Southeast Michigan's seasonal storms, with large trees that can damage homes, fences, and outbuildings. We respond 24/7.

## Milan's small-town character

Milan's tight-knit community and affordable housing continue to attract families and new residents. As the area grows, homeowners inherit properties with mature trees that need professional attention — and new development requires clearing and site preparation. Whether it's a dead ash in the front yard or a wooded lot that needs selective clearing, we bring the same professional approach to Milan that we bring to every job.

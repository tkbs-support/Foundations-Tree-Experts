Dexter is a small city west of Ann Arbor with a strong community identity and a landscape that ranges from tree-lined residential streets to larger rural properties along the Huron River corridor. As a Tree City USA member since 2008, Dexter values its trees — and homeowners here need reliable tree care to match.

Foundations Tree Experts serves Dexter with the same crew and standards we bring to every job across Washtenaw County. We're just a short drive from Ann Arbor and work in the Dexter area regularly.

## Tree care in Dexter

The City of Dexter's Department of Public Works handles trees within the road right-of-way, providing ongoing inspection, trimming, and removal. When a city tree is found dead, dying, or diseased, it's removed and the location noted for replanting.

For trees on private property, that's where we come in — whether it's a diseased maple near the house, a storm-damaged oak in the backyard, or clearing for a new build on a rural lot.

## Tree challenges in Dexter

- **Emerald ash borer** — Washtenaw County has been impacted by EAB since 2002. Dexter properties with remaining ash trees may need assessment and removal.
- **Oak wilt risk** — present across Southeast Michigan. Oaks should not be pruned between April and July.
- **Rural-residential mix** — properties outside Dexter's downtown core often have significant tree cover on larger lots, requiring periodic maintenance, storm damage cleanup, or lot clearing.
- **Huron River corridor** — properties near the river have large riparian species that can require specialized care.

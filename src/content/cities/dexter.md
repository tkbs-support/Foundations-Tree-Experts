Dexter is a small city west of Ann Arbor with a strong community identity and a landscape that ranges from tree-lined residential streets to larger rural properties along the Huron River corridor. As a Tree City USA member since 2008, Dexter values its trees — and homeowners here need reliable tree care to match.

Foundations Tree Experts serves Dexter with the same crew and standards we bring to every job across Washtenaw County. We're just a short drive from Ann Arbor and work in the Dexter area regularly.

## Neighborhoods we serve in Dexter

We work throughout the City of Dexter and Dexter Township, including:

- **Huron Farms** — established subdivision off Huron River Drive with mature trees on generous lots
- **Dexter Crossing** — newer development north of the village with a mix of young and established plantings
- **Brass Creek** — residential neighborhood with wooded lots along the creek corridor
- **Monument Park area** — homes near downtown Dexter's historic core with large, aging street trees
- **Shield Road / North Territorial** — rural properties on acreage with significant hardwood cover
- **Huron River Drive corridor** — scenic road with large waterfront and wooded properties stretching toward Ann Arbor

## Tree care in Dexter

The City of Dexter's Department of Public Works handles trees within the road right-of-way, providing ongoing inspection, trimming, and removal. When a city tree is found dead, dying, or diseased, it's removed and the location noted for replanting.

For trees on private property, that's where we come in — whether it's a diseased maple near the house, a storm-damaged oak in the backyard, or clearing for a new build on a rural lot.

## Tree challenges in Dexter

- **Emerald ash borer** — Washtenaw County has been impacted by EAB since 2002. Dexter properties with remaining ash trees may need assessment and removal.
- **Oak wilt risk** — present across Southeast Michigan. Oaks should not be pruned between April and July.
- **Rural-residential mix** — properties outside Dexter's downtown core often have significant tree cover on larger lots, requiring periodic maintenance, storm damage cleanup, or lot clearing.
- **Huron River corridor** — properties near the river have large cottonwoods, willows, and other riparian species that can require specialized care and careful removal near the water.
- **New construction clearing** — Dexter Township continues to see residential development on previously wooded lots, requiring selective clearing that preserves desirable trees while preparing the site.

## Dexter's commitment to its trees

Dexter's Tree City USA designation since 2008 reflects the community's investment in its urban forest. The city maintains a tree specifications manual that guides planting and care standards. Homeowners benefit from this commitment — but it also means the community expects professional, responsible tree work. We share that standard.

Lot and land clearing is the removal of trees, brush, stumps, and vegetation from a property to prepare it for construction, landscaping, fencing, or other use. In Southeast Michigan, lot clearing is most commonly needed for new residential builds, fence-line preparation, and converting wooded or overgrown parcels into usable space.

Foundations Tree Experts handles lot clearing across Ann Arbor, Ypsilanti, Canton, Plymouth, and surrounding communities — for homeowners, builders, and developers alike.

## What types of lot clearing do you handle?

We provide clearing services for a range of project types:

- **New-build site prep** — clearing the building footprint, driveway path, and utility corridors for new home construction
- **Fence-line clearing** — removing trees, brush, and stumps along property boundaries for fence installation
- **Overgrown lot reclamation** — clearing neglected properties that have become overgrown with volunteer trees, brush, and undergrowth
- **Selective clearing** — removing specific trees while preserving others for shade, privacy, or aesthetics
- **Brush and undergrowth removal** — clearing dense undergrowth without removing mature trees
- **Commercial site prep** — larger-scale clearing for commercial development projects

## How does the clearing process work?

Every lot clearing project starts with a site walk. We walk the property with you (or your builder/developer) to define the clearing boundaries, flag any trees to be preserved, and identify underground utilities.

The crew then works section by section:

1. **Trees are felled directionally** — laid down in a controlled direction to avoid damage to preserve trees and adjacent properties
2. **Brush is fed through commercial chippers** — reducing volume and making debris manageable for hauling
3. **Stumps are ground below grade** — so the site is level and ready for grading
4. **Debris is loaded and hauled** — the site is left clean with no slash piles or brush remnants

For larger projects, we stage equipment on-site and work over multiple days to maintain steady progress without rushing.

## What equipment is used for lot clearing?

The right equipment depends on the site. Our fleet includes:

- **Skidsteers** with grapple and brush cutter attachments — for moving logs, clearing brush, and navigating tight spaces
- **Excavators** — for larger trees, root extraction, and rough grading
- **Commercial chippers** — for processing brush and small-diameter wood into chips
- **Dump trucks** — for hauling debris, chips, and wood off-site

## Who do you work with?

We work with:

- **Homeowners** preparing lots for new builds, additions, or landscaping projects
- **Builders and developers** who need sites cleared for construction
- **Residential and commercial** projects of all sizes
